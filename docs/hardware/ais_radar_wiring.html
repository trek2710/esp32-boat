AIS-radar device — Wiring & Power Guide

Connecting a Wegmatt dAISy 2+ AIS receiver to a Waveshare ESP32-S3-Touch-AMOLED-1.75-G, battery-powered, three wires, no step-up converter.

The short version. The dAISy 2+ runs on 3.3 V (internal switch) and its data pin is 3.3 V logic. The AMOLED board's 8-pin header provides a 3V3 rail and a free GPIO. So the whole connection is three wires from the header to the dAISy AUX header — no 5 V, no step-up converter, no second battery, no 9 V. One 3.7 V LiPo powers everything; the AMOLED's USB-C charges it.

2. The AMOLED 8-pin header (H2)

On the back edge of the board, silkscreened left-to-right:

IO18   IO17   IO16   RXD   TXD   3V3   GND   VBUS
  • IO16 — free GPIO. This is our AIS data input. (IO17/IO18 are taken by the onboard LC76G GPS once you solder its R15/R16 jumpers, so don't use them for AIS.)
  • 3V3 — the board's always-on 3.3 V rail (present on battery and USB). Powers the dAISy.
  • GND — common ground.
  • VBUS — this is the "5 V" pin, but it is USB-only (dead on battery). We do not use it.

3. The dAISy 2+ AUX SERIAL header

6-pin header on the rear, silkscreened:

NC   TXD   RXD   GND   VCC   I/O
dAISy pinUse
TXDAIS data out (3.3 V logic, 38400 baud). → goes to the ESP IO16.
GNDGround. → ESP GND.
VCCPower in when fed externally. → ESP 3V3. Draws ~50 mA.
NC / RXD / I/OLeave unconnected. (RXD would only be needed to send config to the dAISy; we configure it over USB instead.)
Set the dAISy internal VCC switch to 3.3 V before wiring. It's the small slide switch next to the AUX header marked AUX VCC  5V — 3V3. Powering the dAISy through VCC at 5 V while it's switched to 3.3 V (or vice-versa) can damage it. The TXD/RXD/I/O pins are 3.3 V regardless of this switch.

Make and break connections with the AMOLED powered off (battery unplugged and off USB). Live wiring is how 3.3 V GPIOs get fried. Double-check polarity before first power-on.

5. Power summary

  • Source: one 3.7 V LiPo into the board's BAT connector. The AXP2101 PMIC runs the board from it and steps it to the 3.3 V rail that also feeds the dAISy.
  • Charging: plug the AMOLED's USB-C into any charger — the AXP2101 charges the LiPo. No separate charger, no series cells.
  • Budget: dAISy ~50 mA at 3.3 V, comfortably within the board's 3.3 V rail (which already drives the AMOLED + GPS). The dAISy's VCC pin is rated 150 mA at 3.3 V, so there's margin.
  • Why not the header's VBUS/5 V? It only exists while USB-C is plugged in. On battery it's 0 V, so it can't power the dAISy — hence we use the 3V3 rail instead.
If you ever did want 5 V (e.g. to run the dAISy off its own USB or a future 5 V sensor): the step-up module is fed from the battery or the 3V3 pin and trimmed to 5.0 V with a meter before connecting the load. Not needed for this build.

6. dAISy configuration & bench test

  • Baud: the dAISy outputs NMEA-0183 AIVDM at 38400 baud by default — the firmware's AIS UART is set to match. No change needed.
  • Bench test without an antenna or live traffic: connect the dAISy to a computer over USB, open its serial console, and press T (test mode). It then emits a test AIS message every 5 s. With the wiring above, that target should appear on the AMOLED — proving the full chain before you rely on real reception.
  • Real reception needs the VHF antenna and line-of-sight to traffic (see §7).

7. Antenna (for real AIS)

  • Any antenna tuned to the marine VHF band (~162 MHz) works; a VHF whip (~90 cm) gives far better range than a short "rubber duck". Height + line-of-sight to the water matter most.
  • Never connect the dAISy directly to the same antenna as a transmitting VHF radio — it will damage the receiver. Use an active AIS/VHF splitter if sharing.

8. Firmware side (for reference)

The ais-radar device firmware opens a hardware UART on IO16 at 38400 baud and feeds the bytes into the salvaged AIVDM decoder (shared/ais/AisTargetDecoder), which now records target lat/lon as well as SOG/COG. No NMEA 2000, no WiFi — the targets are drawn radar-style on the AMOLED and (later) sent to the iOS app over BLE.
